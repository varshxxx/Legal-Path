Eligibility Criteria for Bail

  • Accused in Custody/Arrest: Must be in custody or apprehending arrest.
  • Non-Habitual Offender: Not involved in serious or repeated offenses.
  • No Risk of Absconding: Must assure appearance for court hearings.
  • No Tampering with Evidence: Should not harm the investigation process.
  • Offense Nature Considered: Gravity of the crime impacts bail decisions.
  • Surety/Bond Provided: Bail surety or bond must be furnished.
  • Cooperation Assured: Must cooperate with investigation authorities.
  • Court Jurisdiction: Application must be filed in the appropriate court.
  • Public Safety Ensured: Release must not harm public order.
  • Special Conditions Apply: Follow additional court-imposed conditions.

How to Apply for Bail

  • Determine the Type of Bail: Identify if it's a bailable or non-bailable offense.
  • Contact a Lawyer: Seek legal assistance for application preparation.
  • Draft Application: Include personal details, case information, and grounds for bail.
  • File in Relevant Court: Submit to appropriate jurisdiction.
  • Attend Hearing: Present your case before the court.
  • Fulfill Conditions: Complete all court-mandated requirements.
  • Obtain Release Order: Secure the formal release documentation.
  • Complete Release Process: Follow procedures for actual release.

Major Categories

  • Bailable offenses: The offender can be released on bail as a matter of right.
  • Non-bailable offenses: Bail is discretionary and requires the court's permission.

Bailable Offenses

In bailable offenses, bail is a right of the accused, and the police or court must grant it upon request.


Eligibility Criteria:
  • The offense is categorized as bailable under the Indian Penal Code (IPC).
  • The accused is willing to provide sureties or meet conditions set by authorities.
  • There is no risk of absconding or influencing witnesses.

Non-Bailable Offenses

In non-bailable offenses, bail is granted at the discretion of the court based on the severity of the crime and the case details.


Eligibility Criteria:
  • The offense is non-bailable but not punishable with death or life imprisonment in certain cases.
  • The accused has no history of serious criminal behavior.
  • There is cooperation with the investigation and assurance to appear in court.

Minor Categories

  • Regular Bail: The standard bail given during ongoing criminal proceedings.
  • Anticipatory Bail: A pre-arrest bail to prevent potential detention.
  • Interim Bail: A temporary bail for a short, specific period.

Regular Bail

Regular bail is the standard bail granted during ongoing criminal proceedings.


Eligibility Criteria:
  • The accused is charged with a bailable offense.
  • The accused is willing to provide sureties and comply with court conditions.
  • There is no risk of the accused absconding or tampering with evidence.

Anticipatory Bail

Anticipatory bail is a pre-arrest bail to prevent potential detention.


Eligibility Criteria:
  • The accused apprehends arrest for a bailable or non-bailable offense.
  • The accused has no history of absconding or tampering with evidence.
  • The court is satisfied that granting anticipatory bail is justified.

Interim Bail

Interim bail is a temporary bail granted for a short, specific period.


Eligibility Criteria:
  • The accused is charged with a bailable or non-bailable offense.
  • The accused has a valid reason for seeking temporary release.
  • The court is satisfied that granting interim bail is appropriate.
